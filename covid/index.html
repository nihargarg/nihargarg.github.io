My Response to COVID-19

Produced and Donated Personal Protective Equipment

In early April, as the coronavirus situation in New York City hurtled toward full-blown crisis, I volunteered my skills in engineering design and 3D printing to support the manufacturing of personal protective equipment (PPE) that are in short supply. Like many members of the 3D printing community keen to assist during the COVID-19 pandemic, I got to work in my apartment producing prototype face mask and face shields by modifying open-source designs to do my part in slowing the spread. What started as a hobby, quickly turned to a duty to help the medical community in whatever little way I could.
If you know someone with access to a 3D printer, please join the movement! Email me at nihar@garg.xyz for the 3D files.

"In a time when so much is uncertain, the need for concentrated relief efforts is paramount."

If you are a healthcare worker or anyone on the front line of the response effort and in need of face masks or face shields, please email me at nihar@garg.xyz. I will do my best to help you!

Visualization of India's COVID-19 Data

COVID-19 case data is the most important data right now. It’s the basis of how we’re seeing and measuring the impact of this virus in the world.

Today, we are flooded with all sorts of COVID-19 related data. While it’s important to stay up to date with the latest information, it is also important to understand the data you are looking at to make more better, more informed decisions. There are many visualizations of COVID-19 data for countries and cities around the world, but I haven't found any beautiful and intuitive visualizations for India's national and state data. Therefore, I decided to create my own interactive visualization to see and better understand how case counts are rising and what kind of impact the virus might have on my community. As states start to reopen, the perception and understanding of the lawmakers and general public must align with the facts.

The data presented on these pages reflect the most recent information the Health Ministry has collected about people who have tested positive for COVID-19 in India. Click the visualizations below to interact with the full dashboard.
